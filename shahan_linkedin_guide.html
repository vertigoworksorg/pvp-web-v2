Key Rules from the Data
Hook ceiling is 120–210 chars. Two consecutive newlines kill the preview early. Keep your hook in a single paragraph for maximum visibility before "see more".
Body sweet spot: 1,300–1,900 chars. Per van der Blom’s 1.8M-post dataset. Longer tanks engagement; shorter feels thin and unfinished.
Native uploads always beat link-outs. ~38% engagement lift, ~42% visibility lift. Never link to YouTube — upload video directly to LinkedIn.
Comments >> Likes. Comments in the first 60–90 min are the strongest distribution signal. Block 15 min post-publish to engage every reply.
Saves drive reshares. Saves are the #1 reach driver. Named frameworks, checklists, and playbooks earn saves. Generic opinion takes don’t.
Dwell time is king. Carousels earn 15–20s vs 8–10s for text. LinkedIn rewards how long people stay on your post, not just that they clicked.

Five dominant storylines in the AI engineering / founding engineer space. Each is a content seam you can mine for months — and your actual work maps directly to all five.

Trend 03

Your angle: you’ve shipped real enterprise AI work and have been hands-on since Jan 2023. You have credibility to speak to both sides without sounding like a vendor.
Trend 04
Founding engineer hiring market shift
Seed-to-Series-B AI startups now explicitly demand "2+ years hands-on with LLMs, RAG, agents, prompt engineering" in founding engineer job specs. Remote AI engineer roles paying USD salaries are appearing in Pakistan listings. The signal recruiters need is shipped agentic work, not credentials. Your CloudFix Finders/Fixers, AI Blog Builder, and QueryLens UX are the exact artifacts that close this signal gap — they just need to be visible on LinkedIn.
Trend 05
Outcome-first prompting + the model wars take cycle
Anthropic’s April 2026 docs explicitly say: delete "think step by step" and "you are a world-class expert" — raise the effort level instead. GPT-5.5’s "tell me your prompt" mockery went viral in May 2026. The "I prompt-injected my LinkedIn bio in Old English to troll recruiter bots" post (May 15) drove massive engagement. Hot-take carousels and video monologues riffing on model-specific prompting strategies are surging — and dying within 2 weeks as the next model drops. Move fast on these.
Evergreen Topics That Always Perform
  • The hard-won lesson with a specific number. "$400K in customer savings in Q1 2025 at CloudFix. Here’s the one architectural mistake we kept seeing across 500+ enterprise AWS accounts." Specific numbers beat round numbers; named platforms beat vague references.
  • Career-stage content. Job hunting as an engineer in Pakistan targeting US/EU remote, salary negotiation as a remote contractor, what "founding engineer" actually means, the CCAT and what it doesn’t measure.
  • "How I’d start over" posts. "If I had to learn multi-agent orchestration from scratch in 2026, here’s the exact 30-day plan I’d follow." Compresses your experience into a gift for people earlier in the journey.
  • The unsexy fundamentals you defend. "Everyone is talking about agents. Almost nobody is talking about evals. Here’s why your agent will fail in production without them." Hamel Husain’s lane — wide open on LinkedIn right now.
  • Public failure with the fix. "We shipped the MVP in 5 days. Then we hit a wall on day 12 with agent context bleed. Here’s what we changed." Vulnerability + technical specificity is the highest-trust format on LinkedIn.

01
Educational Carousel
6–10 swipeable PDF slides · frameworks · your flagship format
24% eng. rateWeeklyHighest-leverage
⌄
What it is
6–10 swipeable slides uploaded as a native PDF document. Cover slide is the hook, last slide is the CTA. One idea per slide. Use it every week without exception — this is your highest-leverage format and the single strongest signal to hiring managers that you can structure and communicate complex ideas.
Topic Ideas

Claude vs GPT vs Gemini routing logic per role. The one prompt change that 10×’d output quality. Last slide: link to ai-blog.shahannaqvi.com.
Topic 02 · Enterprise authority
"How CloudFix Found $400K in AWS Waste in Q1 2025 — The Finder/Fixer Framework" — One waste pattern per slide (idle RDS, untagged S3, NAT Gateway dead-ends, oversized EC2, stale snapshots). FinOps credibility territory. Lands hard with anyone running infra at scale.
Topic 03 · Design credibility
"The QueryLens UX Spec: 7 Decisions That Made an AI DB Upgrade Assistant Actually Usable" — Real design decisions, the rationale, the trade-offs. Closes the "AI engineer who owns UI/UX end-to-end" loop that founding-engineer roles pay a premium for.
Topic 04 · Trending
"Multi-Agent Orchestration in Production: 5 Lessons from 8-Agent Pipelines" — Reference Anthropic’s managed-agents-2026-04-01 API. Quote Hamel Husain on evals. AI Blog Builder as the live case study.
Topic 05 · Founding eng proof
"The 5-Day MVP Sprint: How We Shipped AI Blog Builder from Zero to Production" — Day-by-day scope cuts, FastAPI + Angular decisions, what got deferred. Your "I ship fast" proof for founding-engineer interviews.
Example Hook
Caption hook (max 210 chars)
"I shipped 20 AWS cost-saving Fixers that drove $400K in customer savings in one quarter at CloudFix. Here’s the 5-step pattern I used every time. ↓"
How to Build It
1
Outline before you design. Plain doc. Title (slide 1), CTA (slide N), one-sentence headline per middle slide. If reading just the headlines follows the argument, you’ve earned the right to open Figma.
2
Cut to 6–10 slides. Morphica 2026 data: under 5 feels thin, over 12 loses people mid-swipe. Sweet spot is 8.
3
Design at 1080×1350 portrait (4:5). Portrait occupies ~25% more feed real estate than square. Figma or Canva. Min text 28px. One accent color + white base — pick your palette once and reuse.
4
Slide 1 is a billboard. Bold specific claim + number + subtle swipe indicator ("→ swipe"). No logos crowding the cover. Fraunces 600 for the headline.
5
Add a slide counter. "1 / 8" on bottom-right of each slide. Gives readers a finish line so they keep swiping — removing this one element measurably drops completion rate.
6
Final slide = save + DM CTA. "Save this. DM me ‘PLAYBOOK’ if you want the full Notion doc." Never put external links in the post body — first comment only.
7
Export as single PDF under 10MB. LinkedIn allows 100MB but heavy files fail thumbnail generation and kill engagement.
8
Caption: 3–5 lines, under 150 words. Hook line → one sentence of context → "→ Swipe through" → 2–3 niche hashtags.
9
Post Tuesday or Wednesday 8–9 AM US Eastern (5–6 PM PKT). Schedule in advance. Set a phone alarm to engage for 15 minutes immediately after publishing.
10
Work the first 90 minutes. Reply to every comment with a sentence + question. This is what moves you from Stage 1 to Stage 2 distribution.
Angles
🔥 Viral — May 2026
Tie a carousel to the Claude Code vs Cursor vs Codex war. "I rebuilt our 8-agent pipeline three times — once on Claude Code subagents, once on Codex, once on Cursor + MCP hybrid. Here’s the framework I wish I had on day 1."
🌿 Evergreen
Named frameworks. Naming a framework ("The 5-Pattern CloudFix Audit") drives saves and reposts harder than anything else. Saves are the #1 reach driver in 2026’s algorithm.

Platform-wide

Rules for Every Format

These don’t belong to any specific format. They’re the meta-rules that make every post work better regardless of what you’re posting.

🪝
Hooks: Write them last
The first 120–210 characters carry ~95% of the post’s success. Write the hook after the body — you’ll know what the post is actually about. Test: if this were the whole post, would I keep reading?
🕐
Post time: Tue–Thu 8–10 AM US ET
That’s 5–6 PM Pakistan. Schedule with LinkedIn’s native scheduler or Buffer. Friday 3 PM for humor. Sunday evening for identity posts. Never put external links in the post body — first comment only.
💬
Golden hour is everything
Reply to every comment within 60 minutes of posting. Comments in the first hour are the strongest reach signal LinkedIn has. Block 15 minutes in your calendar after each post. Set a phone alarm.
📅
Consistency > quality
3 decent posts a week outperforms 1 great post a week. Accounts posting 5×/week grow 12× faster than those posting 1–2×/week. Set the bar at "above-average," not "viral."
4️⃣
The 4-1-1 rule
For every 6 posts: 4 educational/storytelling, 1 soft promo (build-update), 1 direct ask (hiring, launching). Most engineers flip this ratio and wonder why nobody engages.
♻️
Repost your best hits
A post that performed well 3+ months ago can be reposted with a fresh hook. LinkedIn doesn’t penalize this if the body is substantially rewritten. Keep a "best hits" doc and recycle.
#️⃣
Hashtags: 3 max, niche-specific
#aiengineering and #buildinpublic beat #tech and #career. Hashtags are for searchability in 2026, not reach. More than 3 reads as spam. Zero is fine for story posts.
💭
Comments-as-content
Leaving thoughtful comments on mid-sized accounts in your niche drives more profile views than most of your own posts. Budget 15 min/day for this. Highest-ROI activity nobody does.
🔒
NDA discipline
Never post specifics that violate your CloudFix/Trilogy NDA. Use percentages and generic stack descriptions. Don’t name Mastercard/Ryanair/Western Digital without written permission. "An enterprise customer with $10M+ AWS spend" is fine.
🚫
No pods, no bait phrases
LinkedIn’s March 2026 Authenticity Update aggressively detects engagement pods, AI-generated comments, and phrases like "agree?" and "let me know in the comments below." It throttles posts that use them. Your edge is real work.
📊
Track saves, not likes
After each post, check saves and profile views rather than just likes. Saves signal "I need this later." Profile views after a post signal the audience is vetting you for a role. These are your real signals.
🚀
Launches = 3-post arcs
Never treat a launch as one post. Have 48-hour follow-up content ready: a behind-the-scenes carousel, a thank-you to early users, a "what we learned in launch week" post. The arc drives 3× total engagement.
